saas-puppy - minimal ruby-on-rails development environment

summary

    this LiveCD of PuppyLinux was baked fresh for the edX CS169 online course on RubyOnRails to atttenuate the initial learning curve for new students who typically have little or no prior experience with virtual machines or *nix environments

    saas-puppy is by no means a new distro - the course software requirements are minimal - the base image is currently raringpuppy 3.9.9.2 and its devpack with ruby 1.9.3 , rails 3.2.16 , and heroku 3.6 pre-installed

motivation

    the current VM image of the full ubuntu precise is far overkill for the purpose of learning rails and at nearly 2GB this unfortunately excludes many students with slow internet connections - even those with sufficient bandwidth typically download it with their web-browser ending up with incomplete or corrupted downloads and must attempt multiple times

    once the download is successful the student must then learn how to setup the VM host properly with the added complication that the image file is wrapped in a tarball which many popular archive tools do not extract properly - these obstacles prompt many students to attempt setting up their own rails environment with little or no experience making it much more difficult for the TAs to diagnose their problems that will inevitably arise

    the result is that typically the bulk of the issues that the TAs must address for the first few weeks are these mostly avoidable errors with downloading , extraction , installation , configuration , and compatibility which are frustrating to students , time-consuming for the TAs to diagnose , and quite outside the scope of the course subject matter - this saas-puppy LiveCD project aims to remedy most of these common issues

release notes for v0.1

    this initial version features a full graphical desktop environment (jwn) and includes everything required to complete the course as well as countless everyday applications you'd expect in an OS (web browsers , text editors , media players , word processor , image editor , screen capture/recording , you name it its in there) - some of these may be removed in the future in order to make the download even smaller but for now you get the full monte - it is based on PrecisePuppyLinux which is a minified (puppified) version of ubuntu precise with full access to the ubuntu repos so you can add any other package you may want (firefox , skype , whatever)

the packages that have been added to the base image are:

  • ruby 1.9.3
  • rails 3.2.16
  • heroku 3.6
  • nano 2.2.6

the package that have been removed from the base image are:

  • none
